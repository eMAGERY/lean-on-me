#AlternativeFlag

While the ever popular Gadsen Flag symbolizes individuality, self-reliance, and rebellion, I thought it timely to celebrate the flip-side of that coin and design a new flag. "Lean on me" came quickly to mind.  Civilization isn't built solely out of rugged individualism, but also through collaboration, contribution, and solidarity as well.

The first version of this flag made its ad-hoc debut during the Women's March on D.C., on the flip side of a mosaic panel celebrating all the various reasons these people were marching -- including on behalf of those enduring Cornelia de Lange Syndrome and other disabilities.

THOUGHTS BEHIND THIS FLAG

The St. Bernard

Long associated with being working and rescue dogs, few other animals could so perfectly embody the selfless spirit of reliability and helpfulness than the St. Bernard.  This flag means to capture and express that spirit in a time of uncertainty.

The Words

Lyrics from a certain song may come to mind... should come to mind, even.  But here, we mean to suggest to those around us... I am reliable.  I am approachable.  I am giving, kind, and invite you to lean on me in times of need.

The Sparrow

Representing 'the little guy' and individual acts of bravery taken in the face of what seem like threatening odds, this tiny bird was added as an appreciative nod to Bernie Sanders for a political life lived on behalf of people other than himself.

A NOTE ON ART THEFT

The original draft of this flag ran afoul of unintentional second-hand art theft.  The original sparrow illustration had been found during a search for public domain or creative commons material, under which it had been listed.  But in the act of creating this site to explain and promote this flag design, it took real digging to find the ACTUAL origins of the sparrow illustration...  to find a living and active artist whose work had been re-published by someone else under a misleading license.

So, I scrapped that version, commissioned a replacement, and link here to the original Artist, to whom I reported the incident.  Art theft is a pervasive problem online and around the world, and needs be fought, though I know not how.
